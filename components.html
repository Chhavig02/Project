Week 7 Studio

Typography Components

Implemented typographic elements demonstrating hierarchy, readability, and tone — applied to the Roots & Routes community context.

Display Heading — H1 Cormorant Garamond · Light 300 · clamp(2.5rem, 7vw, 5rem)

Roots & Routes

Rationale: The light weight of Cormorant Garamond creates elegance without heaviness. Large display type sets the emotional register of the page before the user reads a single word — warmth, depth, cultural resonance. Responsive fluid sizing via clamp() avoids abrupt layout jumps at breakpoints.
Section Heading — H2 Cormorant Garamond · SemiBold 600 · clamp(1.75rem, 4vw, 3.5rem)

Community Voices

Rationale: SemiBold weight creates clear section entry points. Users scanning a page (F-pattern reading) will land on H2s first — they must be large enough to anchor attention and distinct from body text. Line height 1.1 prevents awkward gaps in multi-line headings.
Card / Sub-heading — H3 Cormorant Garamond · SemiBold 600 · clamp(1.25rem, 2.5vw, 1.75rem)

The Kitchen Was Always My Home

Rationale: H3 is the primary heading inside cards and content blocks. It sits clearly below H2 in the visual hierarchy while still commanding enough weight to guide the eye. Used consistently across story cards, feature cards, and section sub-headings.
Body Text DM Sans · Regular 400 · 18px · line-height 1.8 · max 68ch

My mother packed three things when she left Hyderabad in 1983: a suitcase of saris she would rarely wear again, a photograph of her parents' house, and a small tin of spices she had ground herself the night before she left. The cardamom smell followed her across the Indian Ocean, through immigration, and into a flat in suburban Melbourne where nothing else was familiar.

Rationale: 18px minimum body size exceeds WCAG 1.4.4. Line-height 1.8 is above recommended minimum (1.5) — intentional for this platform because many users (F2, Sunita) have reduced visual acuity. Max-width 68ch prevents overly long lines that tire readers. DM Sans's open apertures and humanist letterforms support sustained reading.
Pull Quote Cormorant Garamond · Italic · 24px · Terracotta accent colour
"I feel Australian but I'm always asked where I'm from. I want a space that doesn't make me choose between identities." — Ananya, 22 · Sydney
Rationale: Pull quotes serve two roles: they break up long reading and they carry emotional weight. The italic Cormorant Garamond in terracotta creates a clear visual and semantic break from body text. The <blockquote> element is semantically correct — screen readers announce it as a quote (WCAG 1.3.1).
Category Label / Tag DM Sans · Medium 500 · 12px · Letter-spacing 0.1em · Uppercase
First-Generation Community Resource Cultural Heritage Events
Rationale: Category labels create scannability — users can quickly identify the type of content without reading the full title. Letter-spacing and uppercase distinguishes labels clearly from headings and body text. Multiple colour variants allow semantic grouping (stories, resources, events, culture) without relying on colour alone — each variant also has a distinct border style (WCAG 1.4.1).
Inline Link DM Sans · Regular · Underlined · Terracotta on hover

Learn more about the South Asian diaspora community in Australia, or explore our newcomer resources for recently arrived community members.

Rationale: Links are underlined by default — not just colour-differentiated — satisfying WCAG 1.4.1. Hover state changes both colour and opacity, providing clear affordance. Focus state uses a visible outline (WCAG 2.4.7) for keyboard users.

Hover state

Focus state

Tab through cards above to see keyboard focus indicator

Rationale: Three distinct states ensure that the card's interactive nature is clear through multiple channels — not colour alone (WCAG 1.4.1). The focus ring uses saffron (#E8903A) which contrasts with both the parchment background (3.2:1) and the terracotta border. The translateY lift provides proprioceptive feedback that mirrors real-world affordance of lifting an object. All state transitions use 300ms ease — slow enough to be perceptible, fast enough not to feel sluggish.
