03 — Design Proposal

Design Proposal

A proposed community platform for the South Asian diaspora in Australia — presented to the Connected Communities Initiative.

Site Purpose & Concept

"Roots & Routes is a community platform where every South Asian voice in Australia belongs — whether you arrived last year or were born here."

Roots & Routes proposes a community website for the South Asian diaspora in Australia, commissioned under the CCI brief. The site serves as a central hub for stories, resources, events, and connection across generational and cultural lines.

The name captures the dual experience of diaspora: the deep pull of cultural roots and the ongoing navigation of routes — migration, settlement, and identity formation in a new place. This framing is intentional and grounded in research finding F1 (multi-layered identity).

The site is non-commercial, community-owned in spirit, and designed to be accessible to users across all age groups and digital literacy levels, directly responding to design requirements DR1–DR6.

Information Architecture

The site is structured around five primary sections, each addressing a distinct user need identified in research. Navigation is kept deliberately shallow (maximum 2 levels) in response to DR6 and F2 (accessibility for older users).

UX/UI Principles Applied

The following UX/UI principles drawn from course content directly inform the proposal decisions above.

🔍

Affordance and Signifiers (Norman, 2013)

All interactive elements use clear visual signifiers — hover states, underlined links, visible focus indicators, and button-like appearance for all clickable elements. This is especially important for older users (F2, DR2) who may be less accustomed to ambiguous UI patterns.

🧠

Cognitive Load Reduction (Sweller, 1988)

Navigation is kept to two levels maximum. Page layouts use clear visual hierarchy with generous whitespace. Chunked content in cards prevents information overwhelm. This directly addresses DR6 and F2 (Sunita's experience of feeling overwhelmed).

🔁

Consistency and Standards (Nielsen, 1994)

Navigation, typography, and interactive components remain consistent across all pages. Users learn patterns from the homepage that apply throughout the site, reducing the cognitive effort required to use unfamiliar pages.

♿

Universal Design (Connell et al., 1997)

Rather than designing for an "average" user and adapting for accessibility, the proposal treats inclusive design as a baseline. Colour contrast, font sizing, keyboard navigation, and plain language all serve the full spectrum of users by default (DR2).

🌐

Cultural UX (Marcus & Gould, 2000)

Interface design communicates meaning through cultural cues as well as functional cues. Colour, imagery, and typography choices are grounded in South Asian visual culture to build immediate trust and recognition, without relying on clichéd or stereotyped visual representations (DR5, F5).
