Project Overview

What is this project?

This website documents my research and design proposal for the Connected Communities Initiative (CCI) — a brief to design a community-focused website that strengthens social cohesion in Australia.

I have chosen to focus on the South Asian diaspora community: a large, diverse, and often under-served group navigating questions of cultural identity, intergenerational tension, and community visibility in Australian society.

Design Context

The South Asian diaspora in Australia numbers over 1.1 million people, yet lacks a unified digital platform for storytelling, resource-sharing, and cultural connection.

1.1M+

South Asians in Australia — the fastest-growing diaspora group (ABS, 2021)

3 Gen

Three generational cohorts with distinct needs: first-gen migrants, 1.5-gen, and Australian-born second generation

Gap

No centralised, culturally-safe, accessible online space for community storytelling and resource access

Target Audiences

Community Members

South Asians in Australia seeking connection, cultural representation, event information, community resources, and a space to share their stories.

  • First-generation migrants (cultural anchors)
  • 1.5-generation (bridging two cultures)
  • Second-generation (identity negotiation)

Broader Australian Public

Non-South Asian Australians, educators, journalists, policy-makers, and researchers who want to understand South Asian cultures and contributions.

  • Curious general public
  • Cross-cultural allies and collaborators
  • Institutions and organisations
