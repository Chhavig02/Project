02 — Synthesis

Design Requirements

Research insights translated into clear, traceable design commitments — the bridge between what we learned and what we will build.

From Insight to Requirement

The following design requirements are derived directly from the five research insights identified on the Research page. Each requirement includes a traceability reference to the specific findings and the CCI client brief clause it responds to. These requirements are written as design commitments — not aspirations, but binding decisions that will shape every aspect of the proposal and implementation.

DR1

Multi-voice, inclusive representation

The platform must present content from multiple generational and cultural perspectives within the South Asian diaspora. No single "voice" should dominate. Content must avoid stereotyping or flattening cultural complexity.

Implementation guidance: Story section structured by theme, not country of origin; contributor profiles that acknowledge mixed or hybrid identity; editorial guidelines prohibiting reductive cultural framing.

↳ F1: Multi-layered identity ↳ CCI Brief: Reflects diversity within community
DR2

Accessible-first design — WCAG 2.1 AA compliance

All interface elements must meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards. This includes minimum contrast ratios of 4.5:1 for body text and 3:1 for large text, keyboard navigability, focus indicators, and appropriate alt text for all images.

Implementation guidance: Colour choices verified against WCAG contrast checker; font sizes no smaller than 16px body; navigation fully keyboard-accessible; skip-to-content link provided.

↳ F2: Accessibility barriers ↳ CCI Brief: Accessibility must be prioritised
DR3

Community ownership: contribution and storytelling

The platform must include a clear mechanism for community members to share stories and experiences. Content submission must feel inviting, low-friction, and culturally safe. Submitted content must be framed as community authorship, not user-generated content moderated for SEO.

Implementation guidance: Dedicated "Share Your Story" section with clear submission call-to-action; contributor bylines with optional cultural context; framing language that uses "our community" not "users".

↳ F3: Community ownership absent ↳ CCI Brief: Community members seek representation
DR4

Mobile-first, responsive layout

The website must be designed and built mobile-first, with a primary breakpoint for screens 375px wide. Layout, navigation, and content hierarchy must be optimised for thumb navigation on small screens before being adapted for desktop.

Implementation guidance: CSS written with a mobile-first media query strategy; hamburger/drawer navigation on mobile; touch targets minimum 44×44px; images lazy-loaded for performance.

↳ F4: Mobile primary access ↳ CCI Brief: Diverse abilities and needs
DR5

Culturally safe tone and visual language

The platform's visual identity, tone of voice, and imagery must be grounded in authentic cultural knowledge and reviewed for cultural sensitivity. Visual references must avoid tourist-gaze aesthetics. Language must be warm, inclusive, and non-paternalistic.

Implementation guidance: Colour palette draws from South Asian design traditions (earthy terracotta, saffron, teal) without pastiche; photography guidelines emphasising dignity and agency; written content reviewed for Eurocentric assumptions.

↳ F5: Trust and cultural safety ↳ CCI Brief: Ethical, inclusive, accessible design ↳ CCI Brief: Cultural sensitivity
DR6

Clear information architecture and navigation

The site must be navigable without prior knowledge or instruction. Navigation labels must use plain language. Information hierarchy must be visible and consistent. Users must always know where they are and how to return to key sections.

Implementation guidance: Maximum 2-level navigation depth; active state on current page; breadcrumbs on sub-pages; consistent footer navigation; search functionality in A3.

↳ F2: Older user accessibility ↳ F4: Mobile navigation ↳ CCI Brief: Ease of understanding and navigation

Requirements Summary

The six design requirements above collectively establish three core design principles for the Roots & Routes platform:

I

Inclusive by default

Accessibility, cultural safety, and multi-voice representation are not add-ons. They are foundational constraints that shape every design decision from the ground up (DR1, DR2, DR5).

II

Community-centred, not organisation-centred

The platform exists to amplify community voices and facilitate connection, not to broadcast from an institutional position. Design must support user agency and participation (DR3, DR5).

III

Simple, trustworthy, and fast

Complex navigation, slow load times, and confusing layouts break trust. Simplicity is a commitment, not a compromise (DR4, DR6).
